\chapter{Results and Demonstration}
\label{chap:results}

This chapter demonstrates the complete functional platform through the lens of an actual user learning journey. Rather than presenting isolated features, each section follows the narrative arc of the demonstration session, showing how a learner explores orbital mechanics concepts through curiosity-driven dialogue with AI specialists, hands-on orbit creation, and immersive VR visualization.

The demonstration validates all six specific objectives established in Section~\ref{sec:objetivos} through integrated scenarios:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Objective \#1 (Physics-consistent simulation)}: Orbital velocities and periods match analytical predictions (user-created orbit at 422 km: 7.66 km/s, consistent with ISS velocity at nominal 420 km altitude)
    \item \textbf{Objective \#2 (Natural language interpretation)}: User questions like ``What's a good altitude?'' trigger appropriate tool selection and specialist routing
    \item \textbf{Objective \#3 (Multimodal VR interaction)}: Voice commands, spatial presence, and Quest 3 stereoscopic rendering enable embodied learning\footnote{This demonstration focuses on VR immersion. AR passthrough capabilities exist in the codebase (\texttt{ARHub.unity}) but are not exercised in this session.}
    \item \textbf{Objective \#4 (Real-time coherence)}: Scene transitions, voice synthesis, physics calculations, and rendering execute reliably without interruption
    \item \textbf{Objective \#5 (Modular architecture)}: Hub and three Mission Spaces (ISS, Hubble, Voyager) operate as distinct scenes sharing common infrastructure
    \item \textbf{Objective \#6 (Reproducible implementation)}: Complete source code, documentation, and deployment guides enable independent verification
\end{itemize}

The chapter structure follows the demonstration's pedagogical pattern: \emph{question $\rightarrow$ specialist guidance $\rightarrow$ hands-on creation $\rightarrow$ visual observation $\rightarrow$ iterative refinement}. This complete cycle repeats twice for circular orbits (Section~\ref{sec:iss_learning}) and elliptical orbits (Section~\ref{sec:elliptical_exploration}), demonstrating experiential learning through hands-on creation. The escape trajectory section (Section~\ref{sec:escape_concept}) demonstrates a complementary mode: conceptual dialogue without hands-on creation, showing the platform's flexibility to support both experiential and theoretical learning depending on pedagogical context.
